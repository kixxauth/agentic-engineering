# Design Brief: East Coast Printers Website Redesign

**Client:** East Coast Printers
**Region:** Vermont & New England

---

## 1. Project Overview & Business Goals

**The Problem:** The current website is visually outdated and failing to convert visitors. It does not reflect the professionalism of the business or meet the expectations of today's buyers.

**Primary CTA:** Every page should make it frictionless for visitors to call or email directly. The phone number and email address must be prominent and persistent throughout the site.

**Conversion Model:** Quote and inquiry form only — no direct purchasing or checkout. The site's job is to capture intent and hand off to the sales team.

**Formats:** Mobile first design. Many users will be accessing the site from a mobile device.

**Success Metrics:** The redesign will be measured by a reduction in bounce rate and an improvement in conversion rate — specifically, the percentage of visitors who submit a quote request or initiate direct contact.

---

## 2. Target Audience

**Demographics:** Middle-aged adults in Vermont and the broader New England region. Likely business owners, office managers, or team organizers.

**Psychographics:** Price-sensitive and time-constrained. They are not browsing — they arrive with a specific need and want to act on it quickly.

**Primary Use Cases:**
- Branded gear for their company
- Apparel for youth sports teams

**Design Implication:** Clarity is currency. Navigation must be immediate and intuitive. Users should find their product category and a path to request a quote within seconds of landing. Avoid anything that creates delay or cognitive load.

---

## 3. Brand Identity & Aesthetic Direction

**Brand Personality:** Authoritative and knowledgeable. The brand speaks like an experienced expert — direct, confident, and trustworthy. It earns trust through clarity and competence, not visual flair.

**Color Palette:**

| Role | Name | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | Blue | `#374087` |
| Secondary | Burgundy | `#64324D` |
| Required Background | White | `#FFFFFF` |

**Logo:**
- Hosted at: `https://kixx.imgix.net/nydynamo/objects/4ef58a2cf1c1a35f4478d23076cec5514869f9f1c53325e2ab4b530eab49adf7`
- ⚠️ The logo must always appear on a white (#FFFFFF) background. Do not place it over colors, gradients, or photography.

**Typography:** Sans-serif typeface only. Choose a clean, legible family with strong weight variation for hierarchy. Avoid decorative or stylized fonts.

**The No-Go Zone:** The design must remain professional above all else, even at the expense of being visually exciting. No edgy layouts, aggressive styling, or experimental UI patterns. Customers want clear information, not a design statement. When in doubt, go more conservative.

---

## 4. Competitive Landscape & Inspiration

**Design Benchmark:** The redesign should follow the general aesthetic of Chicago Signs (https://chicagosigns.com). Use it as a reference for tone, layout clarity, and professional presentation — not as a template to replicate.

**What to borrow:** clean structured layout, clear service categories, prominent contact CTAs, and professional use of photography.
